What is Patch the Web?

Patch the Web is an initiative led by a solo undergrad student to create a web where everyone can have a piece of the digital quilt. It aims to provide free web development services to individuals and small businesses.

Bridging the Gap

The digital divide impacts businesses everywhere.

Many small businesses struggle to build an online presence due to high costs and technical barriers. This problem only gets worse as technology advances.

When looking for a service, customers turn to the Internet first. Businesses without a website risk being undiscovered. On top of this, many consumers view businesses without a website as untrustworthy.

Inspired by my love for my future mother-in-law, a small business owner, and the eviction of a decade-old community jewelry store, I created Patch the Web to help bridge this gap.

Who do you serve?

I prioritize customers based in Virginia because I want to help my local community. However, I am open to working with anyone who needs a website. If you meet any of my other qualifications, I will do my best to help you. For information on who qualifies for free services, please see the Services page.

What if I don't qualify for free work?

If you don't qualify for free work, I can still help you. For information on my paid services, please make an inquiry through my business portfolio instead.

What do you gain?

First and foremost, I'm a teacher. I've always felt like helping people is in my nature. I also want to build my portfolio and gain experience in web development. By helping you, I can build my skills while making a positive impact.

How can I help?

You can help by spreading the word! If you have a local business that you love, or a family member that needs to market themselves, tell them about Patch the Web.
